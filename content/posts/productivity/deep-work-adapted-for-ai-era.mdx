When Cal Newport published *Deep Work* in 2016, the smartphone was an annoyance. Today it's an extension of your nervous system. The enemy of focused work hasn't changed — distraction — but the form it takes is radically different.

## What Still Works

The core thesis is indestructible: cognitively demanding work done in a state of intense concentration produces disproportionate results. That's not going away.

## What Needs Updating

Newport's scheduling frameworks assumed relatively slow-moving communication. In a world of Slack, async video, and always-on expectations, the "deep work blocks" need protection at the calendar level — not just the behavioral one.

## The New Playbook

Pair Newport's time-block planning with a "communication budget" — a fixed number of times per day you check and respond to messages. Stick to it for two weeks before judging results.
