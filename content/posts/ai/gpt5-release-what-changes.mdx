OpenAI quietly dropped GPT-5 last night and the internet hasn't slept since. The new model isn't just an incremental upgrade — it represents a step-change in what AI assistants can reliably accomplish.

## What's Different This Time

Unlike previous releases, GPT-5 ships with native multimodal reasoning baked in from day one. You can hand it a screenshot of your spreadsheet, describe what's wrong, and it'll write working Python to fix it without you touching a line of code.

## The Business Case

For founders, the practical upshot is significant cost reduction in early-stage product work. Tasks that used to require a specialist — data analysis, copywriting, basic code debugging — can now be delegated to a well-prompted model.

## What Stays Hard

The model still hallucinates. Context windows are larger but not unlimited. And the "reasoning" chains, while impressive, remain brittle when pushed into truly novel domains.
